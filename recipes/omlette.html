Classic Omelette

Home

Description

A quick and easy classic omelette, perfect for breakfast, lunch, or a light dinner. This recipe guides you through making a fluffy and delicious omelette that you can customize with your favorite fillings.

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon butter or oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Optional fillings: shredded cheese, diced ham, chopped vegetables (onions, bell peppers, mushrooms), fresh herbs

Steps

  1. Prepare the Eggs: Crack the eggs into a small bowl. Add a pinch of salt and pepper. Whisk vigorously with a fork or whisk until the yolks and whites are fully combined and slightly frothy (about 30 seconds to 1 minute). Do not overbeat.
  2. Heat the Pan: Place a non-stick skillet (8-inch or 10-inch is ideal) over medium heat. Add the butter or oil and let it melt, swirling to coat the bottom of the pan. The butter should be hot and foamy but not browned.
  3. Cook the Eggs: Pour the whisked egg mixture into the hot pan. Let it sit undisturbed for about 30-60 seconds, or until the edges begin to set.
  4. Work the Eggs: Using a heat-resistant spatula, gently push the cooked egg from the edges toward the center, tilting the pan so the uncooked egg flows underneath. Continue this process around the pan until most of the egg is set but the top is still slightly moist and glossy.
  5. Add Fillings (Optional): If using fillings, sprinkle them over one half of the omelette now.
  6. Fold and Serve: Carefully fold the omelette in half using your spatula. If you want the cheese to melt more, you can cover the pan for about 30 seconds.
  7. Plate: Gently slide the omelette from the pan onto a plate. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired. Serve immediately.
